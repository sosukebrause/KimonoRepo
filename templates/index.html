Men's Jinbei

A "jinbei" (甚平) is a traditional Japanese summer garment, often worn as loungewear or pajamas. It consists of a loose-fitting, kimono-style top and matching shorts or pants. Jinbei are typically made from light, breathable fabrics like linen or cotton, and are usually aerated around the shoulders making them comfortable for warm weather.

$70

Traditional Happi

A "Happi" coat (法被) is a traditional Japanese jacket, often worn at festivals or as a uniform by tradespeople or firefighters. It's typically made of cotton and features a loose, often long (kneelength or longer), design. Happi coats can be recognized by their bold patterns, vibrant colors, and the presence of a back crest or collar crest indicating the wearer's affiliation or brand

$40

Haori

A haori (羽織) is a traditional Japanese cardigan worn over a kimono. It's a type of outer garment, resembling a shortened kimono with no overlapping front panels, which are tied or clasped together. Haoris can be formal or casual by men and women. They often feature waist or large sleeve pockets and a clasp or tie for the front panels.

$450

Uchikake Kimono

Uchikake (打ち掛け) are luxuriously embroidered and hand painted silk kimono coats worn over a traditional kimono. Used in traditional Japanese weddings and ceremonies.
